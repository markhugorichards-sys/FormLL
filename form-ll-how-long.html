What Affects the Timeline

The main factors that determine how quickly you receive your certificate:

  • How quickly you provide your mortgage offer or TR1 — the solicitor cannot proceed without it. Have it ready to send the moment you book.
  • When you are available for the video call — if you can do the call the same day or next day, the certificate follows shortly after.
  • Whether all owners can attend the call together — if there are two owners and scheduling is difficult, this adds time. Plan the call date before booking.
  • Whether your lender requires specific wording — if additional wording is needed and not communicated upfront, there may be a short delay while the certificate is redrafted.

Completing within a week? Choose Fast Track. Do not rely on standard service if your completion date is imminent. The £80 premium is insignificant compared to the cost of a failed completion or an expired mortgage offer.

How to Get Your Certificate as Fast as Possible

  1. Book immediately — do not wait to see if your existing solicitor can sort it. They cannot issue the certificate. Every day spent hoping wastes time you may not have.
  2. Have your mortgage offer ready — as soon as you book, send your mortgage offer to the assigned solicitor the moment they contact you.
  3. Be available for the call — clear your diary for the call as soon as you have booked. Morning calls mean the certificate can be issued the same afternoon.
  4. Confirm any special wording requirements — if your lender or their panel firm has asked for specific wording, include this in your booking notes.
  5. Choose Fast Track — if you are in any doubt about timing, Fast Track eliminates the uncertainty entirely.
