Barclays, Optima Legal and the Form LL Requirement

When you remortgage with Barclays, Optima Legal acts as the solicitor on behalf of Barclays. When they review your title register and find a Form LL restriction, they are required to obtain a Certificate of Compliance from an independent solicitor before submitting the mortgage to Land Registry.

Optima Legal cannot issue this certificate themselves — they act for Barclays in the transaction and are therefore not independent. You need a separate, independent firm. That is exactly what FormLL provides.

Optima Legal may have specific wording requirements for the certificate beyond the standard Land Registry prescribed text. When booking through FormLL, note that Optima Legal / Barclays is your lender — our specialists are familiar with Optima Legal's requirements and will ensure the certificate is accepted first time.

Can I use the same certificate for a future Barclays remortgage?

No. Each certificate is specific to one transaction. If you remortgage again in the future, you will need a new certificate. The Form LL restriction remains on your title and must be satisfied for every transaction.
